Here is the version that attempts to figure out what approach the X-Plane GNS 430/530 simulator is using. Given how little real information about what the GPS is really up to (like has a new leg been activated or is the new GPS navigation target a “direct to,” or whatever) the results can be a little different to what the 430/530 is displaying (especially with things like procedure turns and holds).  Nevertheless, the results are often pretty impressive and they are always a lot better than showing nothing at all (IMHO).

The general algorithm is to look for a situation where the FMS target is not equal to the GPS target and then to assume that if the last entry in the FMS is any airport, this is where the approach is for, and the current GPS target is one of the waypoints. This is verified by reading the contents of the airport “.txt” files in "Resources/GNS430/navdata/Proc" or "Custom Data/GNS430/navdata/Proc" and looking at all the APPTR/FINAL paths in there to see of the above assumptions look reasonable.

The main problem is that with such limited information the number of possible paths may be more than one, and in this case a number of approximations are displayed. Nevertheless, as new waypoints are found the number of possible paths always comes down rapidly.  Typically the initial number of paths is between one and four, though there are often many similarities in the paths like they all use the same runway, or the paths only differing by one extra waypoint either just before or just after the runway.

Many approaches will just work with no issues, but occasionally one can expect the following approximations to appear:

1, The ND does not known the an approach is being flown until the GPS is actually flying a leg of the approach, so the first thing to notice is even through an approach may be loaded it will not initially appear.

(One important point to note is that the last entry in the FMS must be the airport for which the approach is being flown)

2, The GPS waypoint might lead to different runways, and in this case an “uncertain leg” will appear. In the case below the FMS target is KSJC and the first waypoint is KILDE

                                            

    The following five paths were found that could continue after KLIDE:

 

HIVAK FORUL RW30L ARTAQ ARTAQ

HOSBO WOXAR RW30L ARTAQ ARTAQ

KIRVE TUZGY RW30R ARTAQ ARTAQ

KIRVE COKOR HILUD RW30R ROSTE ROSTE

ZULUP JENES HIVAK RW30L SUNNE SUNNE

 

   So the runway could either be RW30L or RW30R. So, in this case, that label KSJC is used to represent the runway.

  After KLIDE, the next waypoint is revealed to be HIVAK and so the number of possible paths is now just one now just one.

All the paths are now normal, the runway now has the right name (RW30L), and the missed approach waypoint can also be seen.

There is a JVM debugging option that will print out all sorts of information relating to this feature:

 

    java  –Dapproach.debug=1 –jar xhsi.jar

As I said above this whole thing is a little hit-and-miss, and there will be cases where the program will get confused.  The best solution may be to delete the whole flight plan and then reload it. However, anyone who has used the X-Plane Garmin simulation will probably be some pretty odd behavior from time to time anyway.
